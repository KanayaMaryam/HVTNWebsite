HVTN Data Access Survey Results @ HVTN Fall Meeting 2017

Background

At the May 2017 FGM, several HVTN investigators expressed the desire to have a better mechanism for accessing/understanding HVTN data. We have since implemented several changes to improve communication about HVTN data. This includes the initiation of online HVTN Investigator Data Forums, reinstatement of quarterly investigator resource annoucements including updates on HVTN publications, planned posting of more cross-protocol analysis reports, as well as a proposal to improve the existing inventory of HVTN protocols. Meanwhile, HVTN investigators have been having and will continue to have access to all availible protocol team reports including those based on HVTN studies for which they are not members of the protocol team. This report outlines the survey results measuring approval and usefulness of these changes.

Questions 1 & 2: Origins and Former HVTN Participation of Survey Participants (Location and Time)

More than 50% of survey participants originate from US sites, and most have been with HVTN for at least 5 years. For participants originating from HVTN core sites, the greatest number of participants has been with HVTN for 5-10 years. For participants from other sites, the greatest number has been with HVTN for more than ten years.

Question 3.1: Participation in The First HVTN Investigator Online Data Forum

Around 2/3 of survey participants did not attend the first HVTN Investigator Online Data Forum. The majority of survey participants who did not attend answered that it was due to the inconvenient time of the forum, though they plan to participate in the future.

 
Question 3.2: Feedback for the first HVTN investigator Online Data Forum (Sep. 18, 2017)

Ratings for the first HVTN investigator Online Data Forum ranged from 7 to 10. The median rating was an 8 out of 10, while the mean rating was an 8.25 out of ten.

Question 4: Plans to review protocol team reports even without direct involvement in studies

Around 1/2 of the survey participants claimed that they would review PT reports they were not directly involved in "sometimes". Six survey participants stated that they would check the reports "routinely". The remaining nine participants would check the reports "rarely" or not at all.

Protocol team reports from ongoing studies have been availible to HVTN investigators on the Investigator Resources page of the HVTN Members' site.

Question 5: Support for improved inventory of HVTN protocols

It will take a considerable amount of resources to build it, but the network could benefit from an improved inventory of HVTN protocols that includes information on vacinne regimens and immune response data along with a searchable tool.

Ratings for the benefit of improved inventories for HVTN protocols ranged from 3 to 10. The median rating was an 8 out of 10, while the mean rating was an 8.12 out of ten.

The majority of survey participants claimed that they would use the improved inventories at least a couple times per year. Nine others claimed that they would use them as needed. Only two participants cited no need.
